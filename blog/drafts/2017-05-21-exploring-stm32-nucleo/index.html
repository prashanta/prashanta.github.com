Exploring STM32 Nucleo
ARM
STM32

STM32 Nucleo are development boards from STMicroelectronics for their ARM Cortex-M based STM32 line of microcontrollers. Using one of these boards is the most simple, accessible and affordable way to get started with STM32 micros.

I went with Nucleo-F446RE, ordered it from RS Components (Thailand) which was delivered in a week. The board came along with a leaflet that had some URLs and basic information, yes you can toss that out along with the packaging. I plugged in the board to my laptop using a USB Mini-B cable. It turned ON and started blinking LD2 (user LED), this is the default program that comes loaded, a hello world equivalent. Nucleo boards comes with a breakaway panel that contains STLINK, an incircuit programmer and debugger for the microcontroller.

Among other features, Nucleo boards are ARM mbed enabled, which means you can use mbed platform's online tools and libraries with these boards for rapid prototyping (think of Arduino ecosystem for ARM). To start using mbed's online IDE and compiler first register yourself with mbed here; after that head to mbed's online compiler.

(img - mbed IDE)

Next step is to select your board as the target, which essentially tells the compiler to use appropriate libraries when compiling. Head to mbed's hardware boards page which list all mbed enabled boards.

(img - mbed boards page)

Click on the board you are using, in my case Nucleo-F446RE which will take you to the board page. On the left side bar click on "Add to your mbed compiler".
